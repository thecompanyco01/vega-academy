Certified Bookkeeper (CB) — How to Get Certified & Why It Matters

📚 10 min read · Published March 7, 2026 · Target keyword: certified bookkeeper (2,400/mo, KD 12)

If you're serious about a bookkeeping career, certification is one of the highest-ROI investments you can make. It costs a few hundred dollars and a few months of study — but it can add $10,000-$20,000/year to your income and unlock premium clients who won't hire uncertified bookkeepers.

The Two Major Bookkeeper Certifications

In the United States, two organizations offer recognized bookkeeper certifications:

Which One Should You Choose?

Honestly? Either one works. The market doesn't strongly favor one over the other. Here's a quick decision guide:

  • Choose AIPB (CB) if you already have 2+ years of experience and want the most recognized credential
  • Choose NACPB (CPB) if you want to start your own bookkeeping firm (the "Public" designation signals client-facing work)
  • Choose both if you want to maximize credibility (overkill for most people, but it exists)

What the AIPB Certified Bookkeeper Exam Covers

The AIPB CB exam tests practical bookkeeping knowledge across four parts:

Part 1: Adjusting Entries

  • Accruals and deferrals
  • Prepaid expenses and unearned revenue
  • Depreciation calculations
  • Error corrections

Part 2: Error Correction & Payroll

  • Finding and correcting bookkeeping errors
  • Payroll tax calculations (federal, state, FICA)
  • Quarterly and annual payroll filings
  • Employee vs. contractor classification

Part 3: Depreciation & Inventory

  • Straight-line, declining balance, MACRS depreciation
  • Inventory valuation methods (FIFO, LIFO, weighted average)
  • Fixed asset tracking

Part 4: Internal Controls & Fraud Prevention

  • Segregation of duties
  • Bank reconciliation best practices
  • Common fraud schemes and red flags
  • Internal control procedures for small businesses

Beyond Certification: The Advisory Path

Certification establishes your credibility, but it's not the ceiling. The real money is in advisory services. Here's the progression:

  1. Certified Bookkeeper — Credibility + premium rates ($40-60/hr)
  2. Advisory Bookkeeper — Cash flow forecasting, budgets, KPI dashboards ($75-150/hr)
  3. Fractional CFO — Full strategic financial leadership ($150-300/hr)

Certification is step one. The skills you build studying for the exam (adjusting entries, internal controls, financial analysis) are the exact foundation you need for advisory work.

Free Certifications Worth Getting First

Before investing in AIPB or NACPB, get these free certifications to build your software skills:

  • QuickBooks Online ProAdvisor — Free through the QuickBooks ProAdvisor program. Immediately makes you more hireable.
  • Xero Advisor Certified — Free through Xero's partner program. Adds a second platform to your toolkit.
  • HubSpot Academy — Free courses on marketing and sales (useful when you start your own practice)

ROI of Certification

Let's do the math:

  • Cost: ~$600 (exam + study materials) + 150 hours of study time
  • Benefit: $5-15/hour rate increase × 1,500 billable hours/year = $7,500-$22,500/year more income
  • Payback period: 1-3 months

That's one of the best ROI investments you'll ever make in your career.
