Best FP&A Software & Tools in 2026: Complete Comparison Guide

Updated March 2026 · 22 min read · 1,200 combined monthly searches (FP&A software + FP&A tools)

Bottom Line: The FP&A software market has exploded. Modern platforms replace clunky Excel models with real-time dashboards, automated consolidation, and scenario planning. For bookkeepers moving into advisory, FP&A tools are your ticket to $200+/hour engagements.

Why FP&A Software Matters for Advisory Professionals

Financial Planning & Analysis (FP&A) was historically locked behind enterprise software and six-figure salaries. Not anymore. Cloud-based FP&A tools now put powerful financial modeling, budgeting, and forecasting capabilities into the hands of independent advisors, fractional CFOs, and progressive bookkeeping firms.

The opportunity is massive: FP&A software searches are growing 40% year-over-year, reflecting a market shift from spreadsheet-based planning to purpose-built platforms.

Top FP&A Software Platforms Compared

1. Jirav — Best for Fractional CFOs & Advisory Firms

Starting Price: $100/month per company

Best For: Fractional CFOs managing multiple clients, advisory firms

Key Strengths: Multi-company management, workforce planning, revenue modeling, beautiful dashboards

Integrations: QBO, Xero, Sage Intacct, NetSuite, Gusto, ADP

Jirav is purpose-built for the advisory use case. If you're a bookkeeper transitioning to CFO services, this is your go-to platform. The multi-company dashboard lets you manage 20+ clients from a single interface, and the workforce planning module is genuinely powerful.

Standout feature: Driver-based modeling that connects operational metrics (headcount, unit sales, customer churn) to financial outcomes. This is how you move from "here's what happened" to "here's what will happen."

2. Datarails — Best for Excel-First Teams

Starting Price: Custom pricing (typically $1,500+/month)

Best For: Teams that love Excel but need automation and consolidation

Key Strengths: Excel-native interface, automated data consolidation, variance analysis

Integrations: ERP systems, accounting platforms, Excel/Google Sheets

Datarails takes a different approach: instead of replacing Excel, it supercharges it. Your team keeps working in the spreadsheets they know, while Datarails handles data collection, consolidation, and version control in the background.

3. Mosaic — Best for SaaS & Tech Companies

Starting Price: Custom pricing (typically $2,000+/month)

Best For: SaaS companies, tech startups, VC-backed businesses

Key Strengths: Real-time dashboards, SaaS metrics (ARR, churn, LTV), investor reporting

Integrations: Stripe, billing platforms, HRIS, accounting software

If your clients are SaaS companies, Mosaic speaks their language. It pulls data from billing systems, HR platforms, and accounting software to create real-time financial dashboards with SaaS-specific metrics that boards and investors expect.

4. Cube — Best for Mid-Market Finance Teams

Starting Price: $1,500+/month

Best For: Mid-market companies ($10M-500M revenue)

Key Strengths: Spreadsheet-connected planning, multi-dimensional analysis, automated reporting

Integrations: Excel, Google Sheets, NetSuite, QBO, Sage

5. Spotlight Reporting — Best for Small Business Advisory

Starting Price: $49/month per client

Best For: Bookkeepers and small accounting firms adding advisory services

Key Strengths: Cash flow forecasting, scenario planning, management reporting, consolidation

Integrations: Xero, QBO

Spotlight is the most accessible FP&A tool for bookkeepers. The price point is manageable, the interface is intuitive, and the reporting output is professional enough for any client meeting.

How to Choose the Right FP&A Tool

For Solo Fractional CFOs

Go with Jirav or Spotlight. You need multi-client management, reasonable pricing, and integrations with QBO/Xero. Jirav gives you more power; Spotlight gives you simplicity.

For Accounting Firms Adding Advisory

Start with Spotlight, graduate to Jirav. Spotlight's per-client pricing means low risk — you only pay when you have advisory clients. As you scale past 10-15 clients, Jirav's enterprise features become worth the investment.

For Bookkeepers Just Starting

Start with Fathom + Excel. Fathom ($49/month for 10 companies) gives you gorgeous dashboards and basic forecasting. Pair it with Excel models for budgeting. Graduate to Jirav when you have 5+ advisory clients.

FP&A Skills Every Advisory Professional Needs

  1. Financial modeling — Building 3-statement models, scenario analysis, sensitivity tables
  2. Budgeting & forecasting — Top-down, bottom-up, and driver-based approaches
  3. Variance analysis — Explaining the "why" behind budget vs. actual differences
  4. Data visualization — Turning complex data into clear, actionable dashboards
  5. Business acumen — Understanding the operational drivers behind the numbers
