Restaurant Bookkeeping: Complete Guide for Bookkeepers (2026)

📅 March 6, 2026 ⏱️ 13 min read 📊 590/mo searches

Restaurant bookkeeping is a high-demand specialty — 590 monthly searches, keyword difficulty of just 1. That means this is one of the easiest niches to rank for while serving a massive industry (1M+ restaurants in the US).

Why Specialize in Restaurant Bookkeeping?

  • 1 million+ restaurants in the US — massive market
  • Complex financials — most restaurant owners struggle with their numbers
  • High failure rate (60% close within 3 years) — creates demand for financial guidance
  • Natural upsell to advisory — restaurants live and die by margins, cash flow, and food costs
  • Recurring need — daily transactions, weekly payroll, monthly reporting

Restaurant Chart of Accounts

A restaurant-specific chart of accounts is essential:

Revenue

  • Food Sales
  • Beverage Sales (Alcohol)
  • Beverage Sales (Non-Alcohol)
  • Catering Revenue
  • Delivery Revenue (DoorDash, UberEats, etc.)
  • Gift Card Sales

Cost of Goods Sold

  • Food Cost
  • Beverage Cost
  • Paper Goods & Disposables

From Restaurant Bookkeeping to Restaurant Advisory

Here's where the real money is. A restaurant bookkeeper charges $500-$1,500/month. A restaurant advisory service charges $2,000-$5,000/month. The difference?

  • Weekly food cost reporting (not monthly — restaurants need it weekly)
  • Menu engineering analysis — which dishes make money, which don't
  • Labor scheduling optimization — matching staff to revenue patterns
  • Cash flow forecasting — seasonal planning and rent/lease management
  • Vendor negotiation support — data-driven purchasing decisions
