The Complete Bookkeeping Checklist: Daily to Yearly Tasks

Never miss a critical bookkeeping task again. Use this systematic checklist to keep your books clean, your clients happy, and your practice running smoothly.

Consistent bookkeeping isn't about doing everything at once — it's about doing the right tasks at the right time. This checklist breaks bookkeeping into manageable daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, and annual workflows that keep books accurate and up-to-date.

Whether you're managing your own books or running a bookkeeping practice with multiple clients, this system ensures nothing falls through the cracks.

Daily Bookkeeping Tasks (15-30 minutes)

🗓️ Every Business Day

  • Review and categorize bank transactions — Process bank feed imports in QBO/Xero. Don't let them pile up.
  • Record cash transactions — Any cash payments or receipts not captured by bank feeds.
  • Process incoming invoices — Scan/upload vendor bills, code to correct accounts.
  • Send customer invoices — Invoice for completed work the same day. Delayed invoicing delays payment.
  • Follow up on overdue receivables — Quick email or call on invoices past their terms.
  • Capture and file receipts — Photograph receipts, upload to accounting software. Paper fades; digital doesn't.
💡 Time-Saving Tip: Set up bank rules in your accounting software to auto-categorize recurring transactions (rent, subscriptions, utilities). This can automate 60-80% of daily categorization, turning a 30-minute task into a 5-minute review.

Weekly Bookkeeping Tasks (1-2 hours)

📋 Every Week (Pick a Day — Friday works well)

  • Review accounts receivable aging — Identify invoices approaching or past due. Send reminders.
  • Review accounts payable — Check upcoming bills and payment deadlines. Prioritize by due date and discount availability.
  • Process employee expense reports — Review, approve, and reimburse.
  • Review cash position — Check all bank account balances. Flag if cash is running low.
  • Back up data — Export reports or create backup. Even with cloud software, keep a local copy.
  • Process payroll (if weekly payroll) — Run payroll, verify taxes, submit payments.
  • Review uncategorized transactions — Clear out any transactions flagged for review.

Monthly Bookkeeping Tasks (4-8 hours)

📅 End of Each Month (by the 10th of the following month)

  • Bank reconciliation — Reconcile all bank and credit card accounts. This is NON-NEGOTIABLE. Every account, every month.
  • Review and post adjusting journal entries — Depreciation, prepaid expense amortization, accrued expenses.
  • Reconcile accounts receivable — AR subledger should match general ledger. Investigate any discrepancies.
  • Reconcile accounts payable — AP subledger should match general ledger. Confirm all bills are recorded.
  • Review profit & loss statement — Compare to prior month and same month last year. Flag unusual variances (>10%).
  • Review balance sheet — Check that assets, liabilities, and equity make sense. Look for unusual balances.
  • Process payroll taxes — Submit monthly payroll tax deposits (if applicable).
  • Review sales tax — Calculate and file monthly sales tax returns (if applicable).
  • Update cash flow forecast — Project the next 4-6 weeks based on known AR, AP, and recurring expenses.
  • Close the month — Lock the period in your accounting software to prevent backdated changes.
  • Generate and send client reports — P&L, balance sheet, AR/AP aging, any custom reports.
💡 Advisory Upgrade: The monthly close process is where bookkeeping transitions to advisory. Instead of just sending reports, schedule a 30-minute meeting with the client to walk through the numbers. Explain what the P&L trends mean, where cash is going, and what to watch next month. This meeting alone justifies $500-$1,500/month in additional fees. Quarterly Bookkeeping Tasks (2-4 hours)

📊 Every Quarter (by the 15th of the following month)

  • File quarterly payroll tax returns — Form 941 (federal), state unemployment, state withholding.
  • File quarterly estimated tax payments — Form 1040-ES for self-employed / pass-through entities.
  • Review sales tax filings — Verify quarterly returns are filed and paid (if quarterly filer).
  • Quarterly financial review — Compare quarter to budget, prior quarter, and same quarter last year.
  • Review chart of accounts — Any new accounts needed? Any accounts to merge or deactivate?
  • Review vendor W-9s — Ensure you have W-9s on file for all vendors paid >$600/year (1099 prep).
  • Asset review — Any new fixed assets? Any disposals? Update depreciation schedules.
  • Review insurance coverage — Verify business insurance is adequate and current.

Annual Bookkeeping Tasks (8-16 hours)

📆 Year-End (December/January)

  • Complete all monthly tasks for December — Full reconciliation and close.
  • Issue 1099s — File 1099-NEC for all contractors/vendors paid >$600. Due January 31.
  • Issue W-2s — Distribute W-2s to employees. Due January 31.
  • Prepare year-end financial statements — Final P&L, balance sheet, cash flow statement.
  • Record year-end adjusting entries — Bad debt write-offs, inventory adjustments, depreciation true-ups.
  • Close the fiscal year — Run closing entries (revenue/expense accounts to retained earnings).
  • Review and update fixed asset schedules — Verify all assets, depreciation methods, and useful lives.
  • Organize tax documents — Compile all documents the CPA/tax preparer needs.
  • Review and renew business registrations — State registration, professional licenses, etc.
  • Annual financial review meeting — Review year performance, set next year's budget and goals.
  • Update bookkeeping processes — What worked? What didn't? Refine your systems for next year.
  • Archive prior year records — Secure storage for 7 years minimum.

Tools That Streamline the Checklist

  • Dext (Receipt Bank) — Automates receipt capture and expense coding
  • Bill.com / Melio — AP automation and approval workflows
  • Gusto / ADP — Payroll processing with auto tax filing
  • Plooto — Batch payments and AP/AR automation
  • Keeper / TaxDome — Practice management with client task tracking
  • Karbon / Financial Cents — Workflow management for bookkeeping firms
