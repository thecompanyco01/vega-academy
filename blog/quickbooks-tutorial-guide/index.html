QuickBooks Online Tutorial: The Complete 2026 Guide

From first login to advanced reporting — everything bookkeepers and small business owners need to master QuickBooks Online.

QuickBooks Online (QBO) is the most widely used small business accounting software in the US, with over 7 million subscribers. Whether you're a bookkeeper managing client books or a business owner doing your own accounting, mastering QBO is essential.

This tutorial covers everything from initial setup to advanced features — and if you're a bookkeeper looking to move into advisory work, we'll show you how QBO proficiency becomes the foundation for higher-value services.

Getting Started: Initial Setup

1 Create Your Account

Go to quickbooks.intuit.com and choose your plan (or sign up for QBO Accountant if you're a bookkeeper). You'll need:

  • Business name and type (LLC, S-Corp, sole proprietor, etc.)
  • Industry (QBO customizes the chart of accounts based on this)
  • Fiscal year start month
  • Tax form filed (1040, 1120S, 1120, 1065)

2 Customize Your Chart of Accounts

QBO creates a default chart of accounts based on your industry. Review it and customize:

  • Remove accounts you'll never use (reduces clutter)
  • Add accounts specific to your business (e.g., "Subcontractor Costs" for construction)
  • Rename accounts to match your business language
  • Set account numbers (Settings → Chart of Accounts → enable account numbers)

A clean chart of accounts from day one prevents categorization headaches later.

3 Connect Your Bank Accounts

Banking → Link Account. QBO supports 14,000+ financial institutions. Connect:

  • All business checking accounts
  • Business savings accounts
  • Credit cards used for business expenses
  • PayPal, Stripe, Square (if applicable)

Bank feeds automatically import transactions for categorization. This is QBO's biggest time-saver — set it up on day one.

4 Set Up Products & Services

Go to Sales → Products and Services. Create items for everything you sell:

  • Service items — consulting, labor, project fees
  • Non-inventory items — items you buy and resell but don't track stock
  • Inventory items — products you stock (Plus and Advanced only)

Set default prices, descriptions, and income/expense accounts for each item. This speeds up invoicing dramatically.

5 Configure Sales Tax

If you sell taxable products: Taxes → Sales Tax → Set up. QBO auto-calculates sales tax based on location. Review:

  • Tax agencies (state, county, city)
  • Tax rates for your jurisdiction
  • Filing frequency (monthly, quarterly, annually)
  • Tax-exempt customers

Daily QuickBooks Workflows

Invoicing Customers

  1. + New → Invoice
  2. Select customer (or create new)
  3. Add line items (products/services)
  4. Set payment terms (Net 30, Due on receipt, etc.)
  5. Customize with your logo and message
  6. Send via email directly from QBO

Pro tips:

  • Set up recurring invoices for retainer clients (+ New → Recurring Transactions)
  • Enable online payments (QuickBooks Payments) — customers pay 2x faster with a "Pay Now" button
  • Use invoice templates for consistent branding across clients

Categorizing Bank Transactions

Banking tab → For Review. For each transaction:

  1. Match — QBO found a matching invoice/bill (click Match)
  2. Add — New transaction, assign category and add
  3. Transfer — Money moved between connected accounts

Set up bank rules to auto-categorize recurring transactions: Banking → Rules. Example: Any transaction from "Comcast" → Utilities Expense. This can automate 60-80% of categorization.

Recording Expenses and Bills

  • Direct expense (+ New → Expense): Immediate payment — credit card, check, cash
  • Bill (+ New → Bill): Record now, pay later (AP). Link to vendor, set due date, pay in batches
  • Receipt capture: QBO mobile app → snap receipt → auto-creates expense with OCR

Customizing Reports

  • Date range — Compare periods (this month vs last month, this year vs last year)
  • Columns — Add % of revenue, $ change, % change columns
  • Filters — Filter by customer, class, location, or product
  • Groups — Group P&L by customer, class, or month
  • Save custom reports — Create and save reports you run regularly
  • Schedule reports — Auto-email reports to clients monthly

Advanced Features

Class Tracking

Track income and expenses by department, location, project, or any custom category. Enable: Settings → Advanced → Categories → Track classes. Use classes when a client needs to see profitability by:

  • Business division or department
  • Location or branch
  • Project or job
  • Product line

Projects

QBO Plus and Advanced include project tracking: track income, expenses, time, and profitability per project. Ideal for construction, consulting, agencies, and any project-based business.

Budgets

Create annual budgets (Settings → Budgets): enter monthly targets by account, then run Budget vs. Actual reports. This is a core advisory service — helping clients set financial targets and tracking progress.

Recurring Transactions

Automate any transaction that repeats: invoices, bills, journal entries, expenses. Set frequency (daily, weekly, monthly, yearly), start date, and end date. This saves hours per month.

Bank Rules

Auto-categorize transactions based on conditions: payee name, amount range, bank account. Good rules can automate 60-80% of transaction categorization, reducing bookkeeping time dramatically.

Common QBO Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Using the wrong account type — Don't put owner's personal expenses through the business. Use Owner's Draw/Distributions.
  2. Not reconciling monthly — Bank reconciliation catches errors, fraud, and missing transactions. Do it every month.
  3. Duplicate transactions — Entering a transaction manually AND letting the bank feed import it. Use bank rules and matching to prevent.
  4. Messy chart of accounts — Too many accounts = confusion. Too few = no detail. Aim for 40-60 accounts for most small businesses.
  5. Not backing up — QBO is cloud-based but export your data quarterly (Reports → All → Transaction List → Export to Excel).
  6. Ignoring undeposited funds — When you receive payment, it goes to Undeposited Funds first. Group deposit to match your actual bank deposit.
