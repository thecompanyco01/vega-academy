Building an Effective Dashboard

  1. Start with 8-12 KPIs — enough for a complete picture, few enough to stay focused
  2. Show trends, not just snapshots — 12 months of data reveals patterns a single number can't
  3. Include a commentary section — numbers without narrative are useless. Add 3-5 bullet points highlighting key observations
  4. Color-code status — green/yellow/red makes it scannable in seconds
  5. Compare to benchmarks — "Is 42% gross margin good?" depends on the industry. Include benchmarks for context

How Fractional CFOs Deliver Dashboards

  1. Week 1-2 (onboarding): Gather 3-6 months of historical data, populate the dashboard baseline
  2. Monthly: Update with current month actuals. Identify 3-5 key observations. Schedule review meeting
  3. During the meeting: Walk through each section. Focus on variances and trends. Recommend 2-3 specific actions
  4. After the meeting: Export dashboard as PDF. Email to client as part of the monthly financial package

This delivery workflow — combined with a cash flow forecast and financial reporting package — is the core of what clients pay $3K-$10K/month for.

Frequently Asked Questions

What KPIs should a CFO dashboard track?

Core: revenue, gross margin, net profit, cash position, AR days, current ratio. Then add 5-8 industry-specific metrics. SaaS: MRR, churn, LTV:CAC. Services: utilization, realization. Construction: backlog, overbilling.

How many KPIs should be on a dashboard?

8-15 is the sweet spot. Under 8 misses important data; over 15 creates noise. Only track metrics that are actionable.

What format should a KPI dashboard be in?

Excel/Google Sheets for most fractional CFOs. It's flexible, accessible, and free. For real-time dashboards, consider Fathom or Reach Reporting.

How often should I update dashboards?

Monthly for most clients. Weekly for cash-critical metrics. Real-time for premium ($5K+/mo) clients using BI tools.
