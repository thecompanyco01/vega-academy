Why Businesses Leave QuickBooks

Before diving into alternatives, let's understand the common reasons businesses look elsewhere:

  • Price increases: QuickBooks Online has raised prices multiple times, with the Plus plan now over $80/month
  • Feature bloat: Many small businesses don't need (or want) all the features, but can't access simpler plans
  • Customer support decline: Long wait times and outsourced support frustrate users
  • Forced migration to QBO: The sunset of QuickBooks Desktop pushed many users to look elsewhere
  • Limited advisory reporting: For bookkeepers offering advisory services, QuickBooks' reporting falls short
  • Integration limitations: Some industries need specialized integrations QBO doesn't support well

1. Xero — Best Overall Alternative

Xero

Starting at $15/month

Beautiful, intuitive interface Unlimited users on all plans Excellent bank feed integration Strong ecosystem with 1,000+ integrations Built-in project tracking and expense management
Limited inventory management Payroll is an add-on (US) Learning curve if you're used to QuickBooks

Xero is the most popular QuickBooks alternative globally, and for good reason. Its clean interface, unlimited users, and strong bank reconciliation make it ideal for small to medium businesses. For bookkeepers considering the Xero vs QuickBooks comparison, the unlimited user seats alone can save advisory firms thousands per year.

Best for: Service businesses, agencies, firms with multiple team members

2. FreshBooks — Best for Service Businesses

FreshBooks

Starting at $17/month

Best-in-class invoicing Built-in time tracking Client portal for estimates and payments Excellent mobile app
Limited for product-based businesses No multi-currency on Lite plan Less robust reporting than Xero or QBO

FreshBooks started as an invoicing tool and expanded into full accounting. It's still the king of invoicing — if your business revolves around sending invoices and tracking time, FreshBooks is hard to beat.

Best for: Freelancers, consultants, agencies, professional services

3. Wave — Best Free Option

Wave

Free (core accounting)

100% free accounting, invoicing, and receipts Clean, user-friendly interface Unlimited bank connections Good for personal and micro businesses
Limited integrations No inventory tracking Payroll and payments are paid add-ons No time tracking

Wave proves you don't need to pay for accounting software. For solopreneurs, freelancers, and micro businesses spending less than $50K/year, Wave provides everything you need at zero cost. The revenue model is based on payment processing and payroll add-ons.

Best for: Solopreneurs, freelancers, very small businesses

4. Zoho Books — Best Value

Zoho Books

Starting at $0/month (up to 1,000 invoices/year)

Incredibly feature-rich at low price Part of Zoho ecosystem (CRM, email, projects) Built-in automation workflows Multi-currency and multi-language
Interface can feel cluttered Less intuitive than Xero or FreshBooks Smaller ecosystem of accountants/bookkeepers

If you're already using Zoho for CRM or other business tools, Zoho Books integrates seamlessly. Even standalone, it's arguably the best value in accounting software.

Best for: Businesses already in the Zoho ecosystem, international businesses

5. Sage Intacct — Best for Growing Companies

Sage Intacct

Custom pricing (typically $400+/month)

Enterprise-grade multi-entity management Advanced dimensional reporting Strong revenue recognition AICPA preferred financial management solution
Expensive — not for small businesses Requires implementation partner Steep learning curve

Sage Intacct is for businesses that have outgrown QuickBooks. If you need multi-entity consolidation, advanced revenue recognition, or dimensional reporting for fractional CFO engagements, Intacct is the gold standard.

Best for: Companies with $5M+ revenue, multi-entity organizations, nonprofits

6. NetSuite — Best for Mid-Market

Oracle NetSuite

Custom pricing (typically $999+/month)

True ERP with accounting built in CRM, ecommerce, and inventory in one platform Highly customizable with SuiteScript Global tax compliance
Very expensive Complex implementation (3-6 months) Overkill for small businesses

NetSuite is what companies graduate to when they need a full ERP, not just accounting. It's the platform of choice for startup CFOs managing high-growth companies.

Best for: Companies with $10M+ revenue, complex multi-division operations

7. Kashoo — Best for Simplicity

Kashoo

$29/month (single plan)

Dead simple — no feature overwhelm AI-powered categorization One plan, all features Great for non-accountants
Limited integrations No payroll No inventory management

Kashoo is for business owners who want accounting handled with minimal effort. Its AI automatically categorizes transactions, and the single-plan pricing eliminates decision fatigue.

Best for: Solopreneurs who hate accounting, very small businesses

8. Manager.io — Best Desktop Alternative

Manager.io

Free (desktop) / $49/month (cloud)

Free desktop version — no recurring fees Full double-entry accounting Works offline Supports 80+ languages
Dated interface Limited automation No bank feed integration on desktop

If you miss QuickBooks Desktop and want a free replacement, Manager.io is worth a look. The desktop version is completely free with no limitations, making it ideal for budget-conscious businesses.

Best for: Businesses wanting a free desktop accounting solution

9. Akaunting — Best Open Source

Akaunting

Free (self-hosted) / $18/month (cloud)

Open source — full control over your data Modular app marketplace Multi-company support No vendor lock-in
Requires technical knowledge for self-hosting Smaller community than major players Some modules are paid add-ons

For tech-savvy businesses or those with strict data sovereignty requirements, Akaunting offers full open-source accounting. Self-host it on your own server and never worry about a vendor changing prices or features on you.

Best for: Tech companies, privacy-conscious businesses, developers

10. Odoo — Best All-in-One

Odoo

Free (1 app) / $24.90/user/month (all apps)

82+ business apps in one platform CRM, HR, manufacturing, POS, ecommerce — everything Open source community edition Highly customizable
Accounting module alone is basic Complex setup for multi-app deployments Per-user pricing gets expensive with teams

Odoo is less of an accounting tool and more of a business operating system. If you want one platform for everything — CRM, inventory, manufacturing, HR, and accounting — Odoo delivers.

Best for: Businesses wanting an all-in-one platform, manufacturers

11. ZipBooks — Best for Freelancers

ZipBooks

Free (Starter) / $15/month (Smarter)

Free plan with time tracking and invoicing Built-in intelligence score for business health Clean, modern interface Automatic late payment reminders
Limited feature set compared to QBO Smaller integration ecosystem US-focused

ZipBooks is a newer player focused on making accounting simple for freelancers and micro businesses. Its intelligence scoring feature gives you a quick health check of your business finances.

Best for: Freelancers, solopreneurs, micro businesses

How to Choose the Right QuickBooks Alternative

Choosing the right platform depends on your specific needs:

By Business Size

  • Solo/freelancer: Wave (free), ZipBooks, or FreshBooks
  • Small business (1-20 employees): Xero, Zoho Books, or FreshBooks
  • Growing company (20-200): Sage Intacct or NetSuite
  • Enterprise (200+): NetSuite or Sage Intacct

By Industry

  • Professional services: FreshBooks or Xero
  • Ecommerce: Xero (with integrations) or Zoho Books
  • Construction/contractors: Xero or Sage — see our construction bookkeeping guide
  • Nonprofits: Sage Intacct or Wave — see our nonprofit bookkeeping guide
  • Restaurants: Xero or Zoho Books — see our restaurant bookkeeping guide

By Budget

  • Free: Wave, Manager.io (desktop), Akaunting (self-hosted)
  • Under $20/month: Xero Starter, Zoho Books, ZipBooks
  • Under $50/month: Xero Growing, FreshBooks Plus, Kashoo
  • Enterprise: Sage Intacct, NetSuite

Migration Tips: Moving from QuickBooks

Switching accounting software is a big decision. Here's how to make it smooth:

  1. Time it right: Switch at the beginning of a fiscal quarter or year to minimize overlapping data
  2. Export everything first: Back up all QuickBooks data — reports, customer lists, vendor lists, chart of accounts
  3. Clean up first: Don't migrate messy data. Reconcile everything, close old periods, and remove inactive accounts
  4. Use the migration tools: Xero, FreshBooks, and Zoho all have QuickBooks import wizards
  5. Run parallel for one month: Keep QuickBooks running alongside your new system for the first month to catch discrepancies
  6. Train your team: Schedule training sessions before the switch, not after

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest alternative to QuickBooks?

Wave is completely free for core accounting, invoicing, and receipt scanning. For desktop software, Manager.io offers a free download with no recurring fees. Zoho Books also has a free plan for businesses with fewer than 1,000 invoices per year.

Can I import my QuickBooks data into another platform?

Yes. Most major alternatives (Xero, FreshBooks, Zoho Books) have built-in QuickBooks import tools. You can typically migrate your chart of accounts, customer list, vendor list, and historical transactions.

Which QuickBooks alternative is best for bookkeepers?

Xero is the most popular among professional bookkeepers due to its partner program, unlimited users, and strong reporting. For bookkeepers looking to offer advisory services, Xero's reporting capabilities provide the data needed for client insights.

Is there a free version of QuickBooks?

QuickBooks offers a free trial, but no permanent free plan. If you need free accounting software, consider Wave, Akaunting, or Manager.io instead.
