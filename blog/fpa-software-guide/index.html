The FP&A software market has exploded with options, from enterprise platforms costing $20K+/month to lightweight tools built for startups and small businesses. This guide compares every major FP&A platform by company size, features, pricing, and use case. Whether you run a corporate FP&A team or a fractional CFO practice, you'll find the right tool for your needs.

Enterprise FP&A Platforms ($5K-$20K+/month)

Adaptive Insights (Workday)

Adaptive Insights (now part of Workday) is the market leader for enterprise FP&A. It handles complex budgeting, multi-entity consolidation, workforce planning, and revenue modeling at scale. If your company has 500+ employees and complex planning needs, Adaptive is the standard choice.

Strengths: Deep Workday integration, powerful modeling engine, robust reporting. Weaknesses: Expensive, steep learning curve, requires implementation partner ($50K-$200K+ setup).

Anaplan

Anaplan takes a "connected planning" approach — linking FP&A models to supply chain, sales, and operations planning. It's extremely powerful but equally complex. Best for companies where financial planning needs to be tightly integrated with operational planning.

Strengths: Cross-functional planning, scalability, powerful calculation engine. Weaknesses: Complex to implement, requires dedicated admin, expensive.

Mid-Market FP&A Tools ($2K-$5K/month)

Vena Solutions

Vena's secret weapon is its Excel-native interface. Your team keeps working in Excel (the tool they already know), but Vena adds version control, workflow automation, data consolidation, and audit trails on top. This means faster adoption and less resistance from FP&A teams.

Strengths: Excel-familiar, quick implementation, good for teams transitioning from spreadsheets. Weaknesses: Less powerful for complex modeling than Adaptive/Anaplan.

Datarails

Datarails automates the painful parts of FP&A: data collection, consolidation, and reporting — all through an Excel add-in. Your models stay in Excel, but Datarails eliminates the manual data gathering and copy-pasting.

Strengths: Solves the specific pain of data gathering in Excel, fast ROI. Weaknesses: Less comprehensive than full planning platforms.

Startup & SMB FP&A Tools ($500-$3K/month)

Mosaic

Mosaic is built for startups and growth-stage companies, especially SaaS. Real-time financial metrics, automated reporting, and scenario planning with a modern interface. Integrates directly with QuickBooks, NetSuite, and Stripe.

Jirav

Jirav is a solid budgeting and forecasting platform for SMBs and accounting firms. Good for outsourced CFO providers who need to manage multiple client budgets. Integrates with QuickBooks and Xero.

Advisory & Fractional CFO Tools ($40-$100/month)

Fathom

Fathom turns QuickBooks and Xero data into beautiful management reports and KPI dashboards without manual effort. At $40-$100/month, it's the most cost-effective way to deliver professional reporting to small business clients.

Excel/Google Sheets + Templates

For most fractional CFOs serving $1M-$20M businesses, a well-built spreadsheet beats a $2K/month platform. The key is having good templates:

  • Cash flow forecasting templates — 13-week + 12-month models
  • KPI dashboard templates — industry-specific dashboards
  • Financial reporting templates — P&L analysis, board decks

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best FP&A software?

Enterprise: Adaptive Insights or Anaplan. Mid-market: Vena or Datarails. SMB: Jirav or Mosaic. Fractional CFOs: Excel/Google Sheets + Fathom. The right tool matches your complexity and budget.

How much does FP&A software cost?

From $40/month (Fathom) to $20K+/month (enterprise Anaplan). Most mid-market companies spend $2K-$5K/month.

Do I need FP&A software or is Excel enough?

For most small businesses and fractional CFO practices: Excel is sufficient. Consider dedicated software when you have multiple entities, 10+ budget owners, or complex consolidation needs.

What FP&A tools do fractional CFOs use?

Lean stack: Excel/Google Sheets for modeling, QuickBooks/Xero for data, Fathom or Reach Reporting for client dashboards. Dedicated FP&A platforms are usually overkill for $1M-$20M businesses.
